Malaysian Tourism:
A Nation's Comeback

In 2020, arrivals collapsed. By 2024, Malaysia broke records in every metric. Explore the data behind Southeast Asia's most remarkable tourism recovery.

The Collapse and the Comeback

Malaysia's tourism industry suffered one of the steepest drops on record in 2020, then staged one of the fastest recoveries in Southeast Asia. The two charts below trace that arc, from the depths of the pandemic to the record-breaking rebound of 2024.

Visitor Arrivals 2017–2024

Two lines, two y-axes. Inbound (orange) plunged 99% in 2021 to just 400,000; domestic (blue) halved. Both broke all-time records by 2024.

Source: Department of Statistics Malaysia (DOSM); Lembaga Penggalakan Pelancongan Malaysia (Tourism Malaysia)

Quarterly Domestic Visitors 2021–2025

Area height = domestic visitors that quarter. The snap-back after October 2021 border reopening is immediate. Q4 2025 reached 74 million, seven times the pandemic trough.

Source: DOSM Domestic Tourism Survey

Where Malaysia Travels

Domestic tourism in Malaysia is not evenly spread as a handful of states account for the majority of both origins and destinations. Geography, population, and economic activity all concentrate flows in predictable corridors.

Domestic Visitors Received by State

Darker shade = more visitors received. Drag the year slider to trace COVID's contraction across states in 2020–2021, and the uneven recovery from 2022. Selangor and KL consistently lead.

Source: Department of Statistics Malaysia (DOSM)

State-to-State Tourist Flows (2024)

Row = origin state; column = destination. Brightest cells = within-state day trips. The Selangor row and column stand out as the busiest cross-state corridor, reflecting its role as Malaysia's economic and population centre.

Source: Department of Statistics Malaysia (DOSM)

Malaysia's Global Draw

Malaysia's inbound tourism is dominated by proximity, neighbouring countries consistently top the rankings. COVID disrupted that geography when land borders closed, exposing how dependent arrival numbers are on physical access rather than destination appeal alone.

Source Country Rankings — How COVID Reshuffled the Table

Vertical position = rank; band width = visitor volume. Hover any band for exact figures. Singapore holds the top rank every year except 2021, when the Johor Causeway shut and Thailand briefly led. China's band nearly vanishes in 2021 then climbs back to 3rd by 2024.

Source: Lembaga Penggalakan Pelancongan Malaysia (Tourism Malaysia)

Hotel Performance Trajectory 2019–2024

Each point = one year; follow the path chronologically. Occupancy (55%) is nearly back to 2019, but average stay (5.1 nights) is still far behind the pre-COVID 7.4.

Source: Lembaga Penggalakan Pelancongan Malaysia (Tourism Malaysia)

Who's Behind the Boom

Volume records mask a quieter structural shift: the profile of Malaysia's domestic tourist is changing. The traveller base is ageing, and the ethnic composition of trips reveals which communities are driving the boom.

Demographic Shifts in Domestic Tourists — 2023 to 2024

Each line = one demographic group's share of domestic trips, 2023 vs 2024. The 40–54 bracket gained 3.9%, the sharpest rise. The 15–24 and 25–39 cohorts both shrank. Bumiputera visitors make up 63% of all trips.

Source: Department of Statistics Malaysia (DOSM)

How They Travel, Who Goes Together

How Malaysians travel and who they travel with reveals both the country's infrastructure reality and a social shift that emerged from the pandemic years. The mode of transport is driven by roads; the companionship is driven by something deeper.

Mode of Transport — 2024 Share

Each square = 1% of all domestic trips. Private vehicles fill 91 of them: Malaysia's road network makes the car the default for 9 in 10 journeys. Air, rail, and water transport share just one square between them, reflecting limited public transport connectivity to most tourism destinations.

Source: Department of Statistics Malaysia (DOSM)

Trip Type — 2023 vs 2024

Bars compare 2023 (light blue) vs 2024 (dark blue) by trip type. Family same-day trips surged +28%, adding 51 million trips. Individual overnight trips nearly halved, signalling a clear post-pandemic pivot from solo to group holidays.

Source: Department of Statistics Malaysia (DOSM)

The Economic Engine

Tourism is one of Malaysia's most important economic pillars contributing to GDP, generating a sustained trade surplus, and supporting millions of jobs. By 2024, the sector had not just recovered from COVID; it had surpassed every pre-pandemic benchmark.

Inbound vs Outbound Tourism Spending

Lines show annual inbound vs outbound spending. In 2024, Malaysia earned RM107B from visitors while spending RM42B abroad: a 2.6× surplus, wider than the pre-COVID peak.

Source: Jabatan Perangkaan Malaysia; DOSM

Tourism GVA by Sector 2018–2024

Total tourism GVA reached RM292 billion in 2024, up 22% from its 2021 low. Retail trade alone accounts for 53 cents of every ringgit of tourism value, and it is the only sector whose stream never shrank during COVID. Click a sector to highlight it.

Source: Jabatan Perangkaan Malaysia; DOSM

Tourism Employment by Sector 2016–2024

Retail trade and F&B employ 3 in every 4 tourism workers. Jobs peaked at 3.61M in 2022 and have partially recovered to 3.54M by 2024.

Source: Jabatan Perangkaan Malaysia; DOSM
