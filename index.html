Malaysian Tourism 2017–2025

A Nation's Comeback

In 2020, arrivals collapsed. By 2024, Malaysia broke records in every metric. Explore the data behind Southeast Asia's most remarkable tourism comeback.

The Collapse and the Comeback

Inbound arrivals fell 98.8% in a single year — from 35 million to just 400,000 visitors. Domestic travel halved overnight. Yet by 2024, both had not just recovered but set all-time records. The charts below show how fast, and how unevenly, the rebound unfolded.

Malaysia's Global Draw

Singapore accounts for nearly half of all inbound arrivals — every year, without exception. But COVID broke the pattern: Thailand seized the top spot in 2021 simply because the Johor Causeway was shut. China's rebound stands out — from near-zero in 2021 back to 3rd place by 2024.

Who's Behind the Boom

Malaysia's domestic tourist is getting older. The 40–54 age group grew from 23.5% to 27.4% while the 15–24 cohort shrank. Bumiputera visitors now make up 63% of all domestic trips.

Demographic Shifts in Domestic Tourists — 2023 to 2024

Each line connects one group's share from 2023 (left) to 2024 (right). The 40–54 age group shows the sharpest upward shift.

Source: Department of Statistics Malaysia (DOSM)

How They Travel, Who Goes Together

9 in 10 domestic trips are made by private vehicle — Malaysia's road network makes the car the undisputed default. The bigger story is who travels together: family trips surged +28% in 2024 while solo travel fell sharply, marking a clear post-pandemic turn toward group holidays.

Mode of Transport — 2024 Share

Each square = 1% of all domestic trips.
Private vehicles fill 91 squares — air, rail, and water transport share the remaining 9.

Source: Department of Statistics Malaysia (DOSM)

Trip Type — 2023 vs 2024

Family same-day trips surged by 51 million in 2024; individual overnight trips fell by nearly half. The post-pandemic shift is toward group, not solo travel.

Source: Department of Statistics Malaysia (DOSM)

The Economic Engine

Tourism's direct GDP hit RM120 billion in 2024 — exceeding the pre-pandemic peak of RM102B. The sector employs 3.54 million Malaysians. For every ringgit spent abroad, Malaysia earns RM2.60 back from inbound visitors.
