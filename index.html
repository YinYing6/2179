01 — The Shock

The Collapse and the Comeback

Malaysia's tourism industry suffered one of the steepest drops on record in 2020, then staged one of the fastest recoveries in Southeast Asia. The two charts below trace that arc, from the depths of the pandemic to the record-breaking rebound of 2024.

−98.8%

inbound visitor drop in a single year — from 35 million to just 400,000 arrivals in 2020

Visitor Arrivals 2017–2024

Inbound (orange) plunged 99% at the pandemic low to under 400,000; domestic (blue) halved. Both broke all-time records by 2024.

Source: DOSM; Tourism Malaysia

Quarterly Domestic Visitors 2021–2025

The snap-back after interstate travel restrictions were lifted in October 2021 is immediate. Q4 2025 reached 74 million, seven times the pandemic trough.

Source: DOSM Domestic Tourism Survey

02 — Geography of Demand

Where Malaysia Travels

Domestic tourism in Malaysia is not evenly spread — a handful of states account for the majority of both origins and destinations. Geography, population, and economic activity all concentrate flows in predictable corridors.

#1

Selangor — consistently the top origin and destination state for domestic trips every year

Domestic Visitors Received by State

Drag the year slider to trace COVID's contraction across states in 2020–2021, and the uneven recovery from 2022. Selangor and KL consistently dominate; no other state comes close.

Source: Department of Statistics Malaysia (DOSM)

State-to-State Tourist Flows (2024)

Most trips stay within the same state; Malaysians are local travellers first. Selangor is the exception: its row and column are the brightest outside the diagonal, making it the hub of every cross-state corridor in the country.

Source: Department of Statistics Malaysia (DOSM)

03 — International Arrivals

Malaysia's Global Draw

Malaysia's inbound tourism is dominated by proximity — neighbouring countries consistently top the rankings. COVID disrupted that geography when land borders closed, exposing how dependent arrival numbers are on physical access rather than destination appeal alone.

47%

of all inbound arrivals come from Singapore — the highest single-country share in Southeast Asia

Source Country Rankings — How COVID Reshuffled the Table

Singapore holds the top rank every year except 2021, when the Johor Causeway shut and Thailand briefly took over. China nearly disappears in 2021, then climbs back to 3rd by 2024. Both cases show that border policy, not just destination preference, shapes the rankings.

Source: Tourism Malaysia

Hotel Performance Trajectory 2019–2024

Occupancy (55%) is almost back to 2019 levels, but average stay (5.1 nights) remains far behind the pre-COVID 7.4. Visitors are returning yet spending fewer nights, which limits per-visitor revenue despite record headcounts.

Source: Tourism Malaysia

04 — Traveller Profile

Who's Behind the Boom

Volume records mask a quieter structural shift: the profile of Malaysia's domestic tourist is changing. The traveller base is ageing, and the ethnic composition of trips reveals which communities are driving the boom.

+3.9pp

the 40–54 age bracket's share rose more than any other group between 2023 and 2024

Demographic Shifts in Domestic Tourists — 2023 to 2024

The 40–54 bracket gained 3.9pp, the sharpest rise of any age group, while the 15–24 and 25–39 cohorts both shrank. Malaysia's tourism boom is being led by middle-aged travellers, not youth. Bumiputera visitors account for 63% of all domestic trips.

Source: Department of Statistics Malaysia (DOSM)

05 — Travel Behaviour

How They Travel, Who Goes Together

How Malaysians travel and who they travel with reveals both the country's infrastructure reality and a social shift that emerged from the pandemic years. The mode of transport is driven by roads; the companionship is driven by something deeper.

91 / 100

domestic trips made by private vehicle — Malaysia's car culture captured in a single waffle chart

Mode of Transport — 2024 Share

Private vehicles claim 91 of every 100 trips. Malaysia's road infrastructure leaves almost no room for alternatives: air, rail, and water combined fill just one square.

Source: Department of Statistics Malaysia (DOSM)

Trip Type — 2023 vs 2024

Family same-day trips surged +28%, adding 51 million trips in a single year. Individual overnight trips nearly halved, a clear post-pandemic pivot away from solo travel toward group and family outings.

Source: Department of Statistics Malaysia (DOSM)

06 — Economic Impact

The Economic Engine

Tourism is one of Malaysia's most important economic pillars — contributing to GDP, generating a sustained trade surplus, and supporting millions of jobs. By 2024, the sector had not just recovered from COVID; it had surpassed every pre-pandemic benchmark.

2.6×

Malaysia earns RM2.60 back for every ringgit Malaysians spend abroad — a tourism trade surplus wider than pre-COVID

Inbound vs Outbound Tourism Spending

In 2024, Malaysia earned RM107B from visitors while spending RM42B abroad, a 2.6× surplus and the widest margin on record. The gap has never been larger, even compared to pre-COVID years.

Source: Jabatan Perangkaan Malaysia; DOSM

Tourism GVA by Sector 2018–2024

Retail trade's dominance never wavered; its share of tourism GVA held firm even as accommodation and transport shrank sharply during COVID. Click any sector in the legend to isolate it.

Source: Jabatan Perangkaan Malaysia; DOSM

Tourism Employment by Sector 2016–2024

Retail trade and F&B employ 3 in every 4 tourism workers. Jobs peaked at 3.61M in 2022; by 2024 employment stands at 3.54M, still the second-highest figure on record.

Source: Jabatan Perangkaan Malaysia; DOSM
